Kartboard Dash Releasing Soon

Role: Game Designer

Problem

Arcade racing games often lose engagement due to predictable race flow and static mechanics. Players who fall behind early tend to disengage, while repeated races begin to feel similar and lack variety.

My Approach

Focused on introducing controlled variability and dynamic race pacing. The goal was to ensure each race feels fresh while maintaining fairness and skill expression through layered systems that influence gameplay without removing player agency.

Design Solutions

Per-Lap Modifier System

  • Designed a system that selects 2 out of 5 modifiers randomly each race
  • Modifiers dynamically alter gameplay conditions (speed, hazards, control variations)
  • Ensures high replayability by making each race structurally different

Skill-Based Rubberbanding

  • Integrated modifiers into a non-intrusive rubber-banding system
  • Creates situational advantages rather than artificial boosts
  • Maintains fairness while allowing skilled players to capitalize

Core Gameplay Tuning

  • Responsive and skill-expressive kart handling
  • Track design supporting risk-reward decisions
  • Iterated feedback systems (boosts, collisions, recovery)

Impact

  • Increased replayability through dynamic race conditions
  • Maintained competitive tension across all race positions
  • Reduced early disengagement without compromising fairness

Key Contributions

  • Per-lap modifier system design and selection logic
  • Skill-based rubber-banding framework
  • Core gameplay systems and handling design
  • Player engagement and replayability optimization

Design Insight

Early iterations introduced too much unpredictability, reducing player control. The system was refined to ensure modifiers create opportunities rather than guarantees, allowing skilled players to consistently outperform while keeping races dynamic.

Project Pneuma Releasing Soon

Role: Game Designer

Problem

Designing an experience that is both mechanically engaging and immersive while staying true to SCP lore. Additionally, maintaining engagement required a system that adapts to different player playstyles without feeling predictable or punishing.

My Approach

Focused on two pillars: lore-driven gameplay design and adaptive difficulty systems. The goal was to align mechanics with narrative identity while dynamically scaling gameplay intensity based on player behavior.

Design Solutions

SCP-Driven Gameplay Design

  • Translated SCP lore into gameplay mechanics while preserving narrative authenticity
  • Gashadokoro Boss Encounter:
    • Massive waist-up entity reinforcing scale and helplessness
    • Disrupts player through thrown objects and summoned SCP threats
    • Objective-based design: activate four high-powered lights to force disappearance
  • Ensured mechanics reflect themes of scale, tension, and indirect confrontation

Adaptive Ramping System

  • Designed a behavior-based escalation system inspired by wanted-level mechanics
  • Tracks player actions and dynamically adjusts intensity:
    • Aggressive play → increased enemy pressure
    • Stealth play → slower escalation with sustained tension
  • Introduced variation to prevent predictability while maintaining consistency

Impact

  • Improved immersion through strong alignment between lore and gameplay
  • Created varied player experiences based on playstyle
  • Maintained long-term engagement through adaptive difficulty

Key Contributions

  • Design of SCP-based gameplay systems
  • Boss encounter design (Gashadokoro)
  • Adaptive difficulty and escalation system
  • Player behavior-driven system design

Design Insight

Initial escalation tuning caused aggressive players to feel overwhelmed too quickly. The system was refined to introduce gradual intensity spikes, making escalation feel reactive rather than punishing and allowing players to adapt to increasing pressure.
